Glossary of terms


acid - pH-acidity ranging from 3 (strongly acid) to 11 (strongly alkaline) with 7 being neutral.


awl-shaped - liner and tapering to a fine point.


alleopathy - the suppression of growth of one plant species by another due to the release of toxic substances.


alternate - leaves that are staggered , not placed directly across from each other on the twig.


anthracnose - fungi that cause dieback and sometimes death to various species, such as dogwods, sycamores, oaks, and maples.


blade - the flat part of a leaf or leaflet, characteristic of broadleaf trees.


borne - carried or transported.


bract - a modified leaf that bears a flower


broadleaf - tree with leaves that are flat and thin, and generally shed annually.


bud scar - the marks remaining after bud scales drop in the spring.


capsule - a closed receptacle containing spores or seeds as a dry dehiscent usually many-seeded fruit composed of two or more carpels.


clusters - two or more simiar things that occur together.


compound leaf - leaf with more than one blade. All blades are attached to a single leafstem. Where the leafstem attaches to the twig, there is a bud.


conifer - cone bearing tree


crown - the head of foliage of a tree or shrub - this is the form or shape of the tree.


deciduous - shedding all leaves annually.


drupe - a one-seeded indehiscent fruite having a hard bony endocarp, a fleshy mesocarp, and a thin exocarp that is flexible (as in cherry) or a dry and almost leathery (as in the almond).


entire - a leaf margin with smooth, untoothed edges.


evergreen - trees with needles or leaves that remain alive and on the tree through the winter and into the next growing season.


exfoliate - peeling in shreds or thin layers, as bark from a tree.


hardiness zone - a plant can be expected to grow in the zones temperature extremes, as determined by the lowest annual temperature. Other conditions such as moister, soil, and wind might effect the availability of individual plants.


leaf scar - the mark left on the twig where the leaf was previously attached.


lobes - projections that shape a leaf.


margin - the edge of a leaf.


midrib - the primary rib or central vein of a leaf.


native - inherent and original to a geographic area.


needlelike - a needle-shaped leaf.


opposite - two or three leaves that are directly across from each other on the same twig.


palmate - blades or lobes or veins of the leaf arranged like fingers on the palm of the hand.


persistent - deciduous leaf blades that remain on the tree for more than a year.


petiole - the leafstalk that connects the blade(s) to the twig.


pinnate - blades of lobes or veins of the leaf arranged like vanes of a feather.


pistil - the seed bearing organ of the flower. The pistil consists of an ovary, stigma, and style when present.


samara - winged fruit.


scale - small, flattened, rigid, and definitely circumscribed plate


scalelike - resembling a scale


simple leaf - a single leaf blade with a bud at the base of the leafstem.


sinus - indentation between lobes on a leaf.


spines - a stiff pointed plant process; one that is a modified leaf or leaf part.


spurs - stubby, often sharp twigs.


teeth - notches on the outer edge of a leaf.


whorled - having or arranged in whorls


whorl - an arrangement of similar anatomical parts (as leaves) in a circle around a point on an axis.
